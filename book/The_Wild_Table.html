Apple Pie

Inspired by: Tabantha wheat, cane sugar, goat butter, apple
Real-world version: buttery apple table pie
Makes: 6 slices | Time: 1 hr 20 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 double pie crust, homemade or store-bought - 5 medium apples, peeled and sliced - 1/2 cup brown sugar - 2 tbsp white sugar - 2 tbsp flour - 1 tsp cinnamon - Pinch of nutmeg - Pinch of salt - 2 tbsp butter, diced - 1 egg, beaten, for brushing

Method 1. Heat oven to 400°F / 200°C. 2. Toss apples with sugars, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. 3. Line a pie dish with crust, fill with apples, dot with butter, and cover with the second crust. 4. Crimp, vent, brush with egg, and bake 45–55 minutes until deeply golden. 5. Rest 20 minutes before slicing so the filling sets.

Camp note: Best eaten warm after a rainy ride between stables.

Carrot Stew

Inspired by: carrot, milk, butter, wheat
Real-world version: creamy carrot-potato stew
Serves: 4 | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 lb carrots, sliced - 1 small onion, diced - 2 tbsp butter - 2 tbsp flour - 3 cups vegetable or chicken stock - 1 cup whole milk - 1 small potato, diced - 1 tsp thyme - Salt and pepper

Method 1. Melt butter in a pot. Cook onion 5 minutes. 2. Stir in flour for 1 minute, then slowly whisk in stock. 3. Add carrots, potato, thyme, salt, and pepper. Simmer 25 minutes. 4. Stir in milk and simmer 5 more minutes. 5. Mash a few carrots against the pot to thicken; serve with bread.

Clam Chowder

Inspired by: blueshell snail, milk, wheat, butter
Real-world version: coastal clam chowder
Serves: 4 | Time: 40 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 cans chopped clams, juices reserved - 3 tbsp butter - 1 onion, diced - 2 celery stalks, diced - 2 tbsp flour - 2 cups diced potatoes - 1 cup milk - 1 cup cream or half-and-half - Salt, pepper, parsley

Method 1. Cook onion and celery in butter until soft. 2. Stir in flour, then add clam juice and enough water or stock to cover potatoes. 3. Simmer with potatoes until tender, about 15 minutes. 4. Add clams, milk, and cream. Warm gently without boiling. 5. Season heavily with pepper and finish with parsley.

Crab Risotto

Inspired by: rice, butter, rock salt, crab
Real-world version: lemon-butter crab risotto
Serves: 4 | Time: 35 min | Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients - 1 cup arborio rice - 6 oz crab meat - 4 cups warm seafood or chicken stock - 2 tbsp butter, plus 1 tbsp to finish - 1 shallot, minced - 1/2 cup white wine or extra stock - 1/4 cup parmesan - Lemon zest, salt, pepper

Method 1. Sauté shallot in butter. Add rice and toast 2 minutes. 2. Add wine and stir until mostly absorbed. 3. Add warm stock a ladle at a time, stirring until rice is creamy and tender. 4. Fold in crab, parmesan, final butter, lemon zest, salt, and pepper. 5. Serve immediately; risotto waits for no hero.
