Chapter II — Expanded Recipe Drafts

This batch turns more of the game checklist into practical kitchen recipes. These are original real-life adaptations, not copied game text.

Carrot Cake

Inspired by: wheat, cane sugar, goat butter, carrot
Real-world version: small spiced carrot snack cake
Makes: 8 squares | Time: 55 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 cup flour - 1 tsp baking powder - 1/2 tsp baking soda - 1 tsp cinnamon - 1/4 tsp salt - 2 eggs - 1/2 cup brown sugar - 1/3 cup melted butter or neutral oil - 1 1/2 cups grated carrot - Optional: 1/3 cup walnuts or raisins

Method 1. Heat oven to 350°F / 175°C and line an 8-inch pan. 2. Whisk dry ingredients in one bowl and eggs, sugar, and butter in another. 3. Fold wet into dry, then stir in carrot and optional nuts or raisins. 4. Bake 28–35 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean. 5. Cool before slicing. Add cream cheese frosting if you want the stable-inn version.

Crab Omelet with Rice

Inspired by: rice, egg, salt, crab
Real-world version: crab fried rice under a soft omelet
Serves: 2 | Time: 25 min | Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients - 2 cups cooked rice, chilled if possible - 4 eggs - 4 oz crab meat - 1 tbsp butter - 1 tsp soy sauce - 1 scallion, sliced - Salt and pepper

Method 1. Stir-fry rice in butter until hot and lightly toasted. 2. Fold in crab, soy sauce, scallion, salt, and pepper. 3. Beat eggs with a pinch of salt. 4. Cook eggs in a nonstick pan into two soft omelets. 5. Slide each omelet over a mound of crab rice.

Crab Stir-Fry

Inspired by: crab and spice
Real-world version: spicy garlic crab with vegetables
Serves: 2–3 | Time: 20 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 8 oz crab meat or cooked crab pieces - 1 bell pepper, sliced - 1 small onion, sliced - 2 garlic cloves, minced - 1 tbsp oil - 1 tbsp soy sauce - 1 tsp curry powder or chili crisp - 1 tsp honey or sugar - Lime wedge

Method 1. Heat oil in a wide skillet. Stir-fry onion and pepper until browned at the edges. 2. Add garlic and curry powder or chili crisp for 30 seconds. 3. Add crab, soy sauce, and honey. Toss until just hot. 4. Finish with lime and serve over rice.

Cream of Mushroom Soup

Inspired by: mushroom, milk, salt, herbs
Real-world version: rustic creamy mushroom soup
Serves: 4 | Time: 35 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 lb mushrooms, sliced - 2 tbsp butter - 1 onion, diced - 2 garlic cloves, minced - 2 tbsp flour - 3 cups stock - 1 cup milk or cream - 1 tsp thyme - Salt and pepper

Method 1. Brown mushrooms in butter until their liquid cooks off. 2. Add onion, garlic, thyme, salt, and pepper; cook 5 minutes. 3. Stir in flour, then slowly add stock. 4. Simmer 15 minutes, then add milk or cream. 5. Blend part of the soup if you want it thicker.

Cream of Vegetable Soup

Inspired by: vegetables, milk, salt
Real-world version: creamy garden vegetable soup
Serves: 4 | Time: 35 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 tbsp butter - 1 onion, diced - 2 carrots, diced - 2 cups chopped vegetables, such as zucchini, greens, peas, or squash - 2 tbsp flour - 3 cups vegetable stock - 1 cup milk - Salt, pepper, and parsley

Method 1. Cook onion and carrots in butter until softened. 2. Add remaining vegetables and cook 3 minutes. 3. Stir in flour, then stock. Simmer until vegetables are tender. 4. Add milk and warm gently. 5. Season and finish with parsley.

Creamy Heart Soup

Inspired by: radish, hydromelon, voltfruit, milk
Real-world version: chilled melon-radish soup with yogurt
Serves: 4 small bowls | Time: 15 min plus chilling | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 3 cups ripe melon, cubed - 4 radishes, chopped - 1/2 cup plain yogurt - 1 tbsp honey - 1 tbsp lime juice - Pinch of salt - Mint or basil

Method 1. Blend melon, radish, yogurt, honey, lime, and salt until smooth. 2. Chill at least 30 minutes. 3. Serve cold with torn mint or basil. 4. For a sweeter dessert version, add more honey and skip half the radish.

Creamy Meat Soup

Inspired by: meat, radish, milk, salt
Real-world version: creamy beef and root vegetable soup
Serves: 4 | Time: 1 hr | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 3/4 lb stew beef or ground beef - 1 tbsp oil - 1 onion, diced - 2 cups diced radish, turnip, or potato - 3 cups beef stock - 1 cup milk - 1 tbsp flour mixed with 2 tbsp water - Salt, pepper, thyme

Method 1. Brown beef in oil, then add onion and cook until soft. 2. Add root vegetables, stock, thyme, salt, and pepper. 3. Simmer 35–40 minutes, until tender. 4. Stir in milk and flour slurry. Simmer until lightly thickened.

Creamy Seafood Soup

Inspired by: seafood, radish, milk, salt
Real-world version: gentle fish chowder
Serves: 4 | Time: 35 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 lb white fish, cut into chunks - 2 tbsp butter - 1 leek or onion, sliced - 2 cups diced potato or daikon - 3 cups seafood or vegetable stock - 1 cup milk - Salt, pepper, dill or parsley

Method 1. Cook leek in butter until soft. 2. Add potato or daikon, stock, salt, and pepper. Simmer until tender. 3. Add fish and cook 4–6 minutes, just until it flakes. 4. Stir in milk and herbs. Do not boil after adding milk.

Curry Pilaf

Inspired by: rice, butter, egg, spice
Real-world version: golden curry rice pilaf
Serves: 4 | Time: 35 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 1/2 cups rice - 2 tbsp butter - 1 small onion, diced - 2 tsp curry powder - 2 1/4 cups chicken or vegetable stock - 1/2 tsp salt - 2 boiled eggs or fried eggs, to serve

Method 1. Rinse rice until the water runs mostly clear. 2. Cook onion in butter, then stir in curry powder and rice. 3. Add stock and salt. Cover and simmer 15 minutes. 4. Rest 10 minutes off heat, then fluff. 5. Serve with sliced boiled eggs or a fried egg.

Curry Rice

Inspired by: rice and Goron spice
Real-world version: Japanese-style curry rice
Serves: 4 | Time: 45 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 cups cooked rice - 1 tbsp oil - 1 onion, sliced - 2 carrots, chopped - 1 potato, chopped - 2 cups stock or water - 2 blocks Japanese curry roux, or 2 tbsp curry powder plus 1 tbsp flour - Optional: peas, chicken, or tofu

Method 1. Cook onion in oil until golden. 2. Add carrots, potato, optional protein, and stock. Simmer until tender. 3. Stir in curry roux until glossy and thick. 4. Spoon over hot rice.

Dubious Food

Inspired by: failed cooking experiments
Real-world version: intentionally chaotic loaded hash
Serves: 2 | Time: 25 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 cups cooked potatoes or leftover fries - 1/2 cup chopped cooked meat, beans, or mushrooms - 1/2 cup odd leftover vegetables - 2 tbsp sauce: ketchup, curry sauce, gravy, or hot sauce - 2 eggs - Salt and pepper

Method 1. Crisp potatoes in a skillet. 2. Add meat or mushrooms and vegetables; cook until hot. 3. Stir in your sauce of choice. 4. Top with fried eggs. 5. Name it only after eating it.

Egg Pudding

Inspired by: egg, milk, sugar
Real-world version: baked vanilla custard
Serves: 4 | Time: 50 min plus chilling | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 cups milk - 3 eggs - 1/3 cup sugar - 1 tsp vanilla - Pinch of salt - Optional: caramel sauce

Method 1. Heat oven to 325°F / 160°C. 2. Warm milk until steaming, not boiling. 3. Whisk eggs, sugar, vanilla, and salt. Slowly whisk in warm milk. 4. Pour into ramekins and bake in a hot-water bath 30–35 minutes. 5. Chill and serve with caramel.

Egg Tart

Inspired by: egg, wheat, sugar, butter
Real-world version: simple custard tartlets
Makes: 8 tartlets | Time: 1 hr | Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients - 1 sheet pie dough or puff pastry - 3 eggs - 1/3 cup sugar - 3/4 cup milk - 1/4 cup cream - 1 tsp vanilla - Pinch of salt

Method 1. Heat oven to 375°F / 190°C. 2. Cut pastry into muffin tin rounds and press into cups. 3. Whisk eggs, sugar, milk, cream, vanilla, and salt. 4. Fill pastry shells three-quarters full. 5. Bake 22–28 minutes, until custard is set and pastry is golden.

Energizing Honey Candy

Inspired by: courser bee honey
Real-world version: honey-lemon hard candy
Makes: about 30 pieces | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients - 1 cup sugar - 1/2 cup honey - 2 tbsp water - 1 tbsp lemon juice - Pinch of salt - Powdered sugar for dusting

Method 1. Line a tray with parchment and dust lightly with powdered sugar. 2. Boil sugar, honey, water, lemon, and salt to 300°F / 149°C. 3. Pour carefully onto the tray. 4. Cool, crack into pieces, and dust to prevent sticking.

Fish Pie

Inspired by: wheat, butter, salt, fish
Real-world version: creamy fish pot pie
Serves: 4 | Time: 1 hr | Difficulty: Medium

Ingredients - 1 pie crust or puff pastry sheet - 1 lb white fish, chunks - 2 tbsp butter - 1 small onion, diced - 2 tbsp flour - 1 cup milk - 1/2 cup stock - 1 cup peas or chopped greens - Salt, pepper, dill

Method 1. Heat oven to 400°F / 200°C. 2. Cook onion in butter, stir in flour, then whisk in milk and stock. 3. Add fish, peas, dill, salt, and pepper. 4. Pour into a baking dish, cover with pastry, vent, and bake 25–30 minutes.

Fish Skewer

Inspired by: simple cooked fish
Real-world version: lemon-herb fish kebabs
Serves: 4 | Time: 25 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 1/2 lb firm white fish or salmon, cubed - 2 tbsp olive oil - 1 tbsp lemon juice - 1 garlic clove, grated - 1 tsp chopped dill or parsley - Salt and pepper

Method 1. Toss fish with oil, lemon, garlic, herbs, salt, and pepper. 2. Thread onto skewers. 3. Grill or broil 6–8 minutes, turning once, until just cooked. 4. Serve with rice or flatbread.

Fish and Mushroom Skewer

Inspired by: fish plus mushrooms
Real-world version: salmon-mushroom skewers with miso butter
Serves: 4 | Time: 30 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 lb salmon or firm fish, cubed - 8 oz mushrooms - 2 tbsp softened butter - 1 tbsp miso or soy sauce - 1 tsp honey - Lemon wedges

Method 1. Mix butter with miso or soy sauce and honey. 2. Skewer fish and mushrooms alternately. 3. Broil or grill 8–10 minutes, brushing with miso butter. 4. Finish with lemon.

Fragrant Mushroom Sauté

Inspired by: mushrooms and aromatic spice
Real-world version: spiced butter mushrooms
Serves: 3–4 | Time: 20 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 lb mushrooms, halved - 2 tbsp butter - 1 garlic clove, minced - 1/2 tsp cumin - 1/2 tsp smoked paprika - Salt and pepper - Parsley

Method 1. Brown mushrooms in a dry skillet until they release and reabsorb moisture. 2. Add butter, garlic, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper. 3. Cook until glossy and browned. 4. Finish with parsley.

Fried Bananas

Inspired by: mighty bananas
Real-world version: caramelized skillet bananas
Serves: 2 | Time: 10 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 bananas, split lengthwise - 1 tbsp butter - 1 tbsp brown sugar - Pinch cinnamon - Pinch salt - Optional: yogurt or ice cream

Method 1. Melt butter in a skillet over medium heat. 2. Add bananas cut-side down and sprinkle with sugar, cinnamon, and salt. 3. Cook 2–3 minutes until caramelized. 4. Serve immediately.

Fried Egg and Rice

Inspired by: egg and rice
Real-world version: crispy rice with fried egg
Serves: 1–2 | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 cups cooked rice - 1 tbsp oil or butter - 2 eggs - 1 tsp soy sauce - 1 scallion, sliced - Salt and pepper

Method 1. Crisp rice in oil or butter until hot and lightly browned. 2. Season with soy sauce, salt, and pepper. 3. Fry eggs to your liking. 4. Top rice with eggs and scallion.

Fried Wild Greens

Inspired by: wild herbs and greens
Real-world version: garlic sautéed greens
Serves: 4 | Time: 15 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 large bunch kale, chard, spinach, or mixed greens - 1 tbsp olive oil - 1 garlic clove, sliced - Pinch chili flakes - Salt - Lemon juice

Method 1. Heat oil and toast garlic until fragrant. 2. Add greens by handfuls and toss until wilted. 3. Season with salt and chili flakes. 4. Finish with lemon.

Copious Fried Wild Greens

Inspired by: a full bundle of mixed greens
Real-world version: big skillet greens with herbs and seeds
Serves: 4–6 | Time: 20 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 2 large bunches mixed greens - 2 tbsp olive oil - 2 garlic cloves, sliced - 1/4 cup chopped herbs - 2 tbsp toasted sesame or pumpkin seeds - Salt, pepper, lemon

Method 1. Cook garlic in oil until fragrant. 2. Add greens in batches until wilted. 3. Stir in herbs, seeds, salt, and pepper. 4. Finish with lemon and serve piled high.

Fruit and Mushroom Mix

Inspired by: fruit plus mushrooms
Real-world version: warm mushroom-apple side dish
Serves: 4 | Time: 25 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 12 oz mushrooms, sliced - 1 apple or pear, sliced - 2 tbsp butter - 1 tsp honey - 1 tsp vinegar or lemon juice - Salt, pepper, thyme

Method 1. Brown mushrooms in butter with thyme, salt, and pepper. 2. Add apple or pear slices and cook until just tender. 3. Stir in honey and vinegar. 4. Serve beside roast meat, rice, or bread.

Fruit Pie

Inspired by: fruit, wheat, butter, sugar
Real-world version: flexible berry-or-stone-fruit pie
Makes: 1 pie | Time: 1 hr 20 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 double pie crust - 5 cups sliced fruit or berries - 1/2 cup sugar - 3 tbsp cornstarch or flour - 1 tbsp lemon juice - Pinch of salt - 1 egg, beaten

Method 1. Heat oven to 400°F / 200°C. 2. Toss fruit with sugar, starch, lemon, and salt. 3. Fill crust, top with second crust, crimp, vent, and brush with egg. 4. Bake 45–55 minutes until bubbling.

Fruitcake

Inspired by: fruit, wheat, sugar
Real-world version: bright tea fruitcake, not the scary holiday brick
Makes: 1 loaf | Time: 1 hr 15 min | Difficulty: Easy

Ingredients - 1 1/2 cups flour - 1 tsp baking powder - 1/2 tsp salt - 1/2 cup butter - 2/3 cup sugar - 2 eggs - 1/2 cup milk - 1 cup chopped dried or fresh fruit - 1 tsp citrus zest

Method 1. Heat oven to 350°F / 175°C and line a loaf pan. 2. Cream butter and sugar, then beat in eggs. 3. Add dry ingredients alternating with milk. 4. Fold in fruit and zest. 5. Bake 45–55 minutes.
